Automated Colorization

Give you a black-and-white picture, can you color it? For some objects such as sky and glass, it's easy to guess what their colors should be. For other objects, like cars and shirts, it's entirely upto your imagination to fill in the possible colors. This task can not only demonstrate neural networks' ability of visual understanding but also the ability of imagination.

Learning Representations for Automatic Colorization

A deep neural architecture (VGG) used for image classification is altered to an end-to-end system for this task. Instead of outputing image labels, the end-to-end system is trained to output predicted hue and chroma distribution.
